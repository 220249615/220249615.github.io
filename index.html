According to the overall public security situation in Hong Kong in 2022.

The number of fraud cases in Hong Kong showed a significant increase in 2022, with a total of 27,923 cases, representing a rise of 45.1% (+8,674 cases) compared to the previous year. Over 70% of these cases were related to online scams. The increase is mainly attributed to:

  • Online shopping scams (8,735 cases)
  • Job search scams (2,996 cases)
  • Investment scams (2,850 cases).
  • Telephone scams (2,831 cases)

The proliferation of various types of online fraud has resulted in a substantial portion of the population being duped, compromising their personal information, and incurring financial losses.

Telecommunication Scams

Telecom fraud refers to criminal activities in which perpetrators use telephone, internet, and text message platforms to disseminate false information, set up scams, and remotely and non-contactingly deceive victims into transferring funds or making payments resulting in illegal appropriation of other people's assets.

Email Scams

In instances of email fraud, perpetrators utilize email headers to conceal their identity and masquerade as legitimate senders. Due to the fraudulent emails appearing to originate from genuine sources, recipients may be deceived into divulging sensitive information, clicking on malicious links, or undertaking other actions that they would not typically engage in. Consequently, email fraud is frequently employed in phishing attacks.

Social Media Scams

Social media fraud involves criminals using social media to deceive individuals by impersonating legitimate identities or creating fake ones. They often pose as well-known brands or public figures to gain trust and use various tactics such as sending fake messages, stealing personal information, or demanding ransom payments. Incidents of social media fraud have increased globally in recent years.

Stay Vigilant

Online fraud often takes advantage of people's carelessness, so increasing awareness of online security is the first step in preventing it. Specifically, people need to learn how to identify online fraud, such as

  • Checking website certificates
  • Verifying URLs (http://XXXXXXX OR https://XXXXXX)
  • Stay vigilant and avoid trusting strangers or unverified sources too much (Any unverified Email or Phone Call)

If there is any suspicious behavior, it should be stopped immediately and its authenticity should be verified as soon as possible.

Protect Sensitive Personal Information

For Example:

  • ID numbers
  • Bank account information
  • Personal address
  • Control the use of cookies(blocking unnecessary cookies as much as possible helps limit the number of third parties tracking personal online activity)

Avoid disclosing such information to strangers and ensure the protection measures of devices such as computers and phones.

Also, passwords are an important part of online security, and using strong passwords can effectively prevent online fraud. People should use strong passwords and not use the same password on different websites.

Learn How To Say NO

When faced with suspicious information, we must:

  • Avoid clicking on any suspicious links
  • Use antivirus software to protect your computer
  • Confirming whether the Website or Email has security certification
  • Be alert to recent phishing attacks

Rejecting suspicious messages is an important step in protecting your personal information and devices. If you feel unsure or uneasy about a message, the best course of action is to delete it and take appropriate measures to protect your information and devices.

Strengthen Online Security Education and Training

Increase public awareness and vigilance of online fraud, and reduce the number of victims and losses.

How to educate and train:

  • Providing cybersecurity knowledge is the basis for preventing online scams
    (How to identify online scam)
  • Provide transaction security knowledge, online transactions often have scam risks, so the public should choose legal websites for transactions and pay attention to the security details of transactions (Checking transaction details)
  • Emphasize the importance of protecting personal information, protecting personal information is one of the important measures to prevent online scams. The public should avoid disclosing personal information to strangers, ensure the protection measures of devices such as computers and phones, and learn how to identify and respond to identity theft and other issues.
  • Provide actual case studies, providing actual online scam case studies can help the public better understand the harm and impact of online scams, as well as how to avoid becoming victims.

Hong Kong Police Force

https://www.adcc.gov.hk/zh-hk/home.html>

The Hong Kong Police Force is responsible for combating all types of criminal activities, including internet scams. The Cyber Security and Technology Crime Bureau (C3A) of the police force is responsible for investigating internet scam cases and cooperating with other international law enforcement agencies to combat cross-border criminal activities.

Consumer Council of Hong Kong

https://www.consumer.org.hk/en

The Consumer Council of Hong Kong is responsible for protecting consumer rights and providing dispute resolution services between consumers and businesses. The Consumer Council also provides warnings about internet scams to the public and offers advice on how to prevent them.

Hong Kong Monetary Authority

https://www.hkma.gov.hk/eng/

The Hong Kong Monetary Authority is responsible for regulating Hong Kong's financial industry, including banks, insurance, securities, and more. The Hong Kong Monetary Authority also regulates virtual asset exchanges and develops related regulatory policies. In terms of internet scams, the Hong Kong Monetary Authority provides warnings about virtual assets and requires virtual asset exchanges to strengthen their anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing regulatory measures.

Hong Kong Science and Technology Parks Corporation

https://www.hkstp.org/

The Hong Kong Science and Technology Parks Corporation is an organization responsible for promoting technological innovation. It has several accelerators and incubation centers to support startups. The corporation also holds events related to internet security and information security to raise public awareness and understanding about these issues.
